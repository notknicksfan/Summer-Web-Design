Ozean

Founded in 2021, Ozean focuses on informing others on the effects fish farming has on our planet. Fish has become an cheap and accessible source of protein but at the cost of our planet. With the current state of fish farming, it is unsustainable and together we can change that slowly.

The demand for popular fish like salmon and tilapia has resulted in an alarming increase of fish farms around the world. In order to build fish farms, farmers have to disrupt local habitats and ecosystems. Habitats act as a barrier against natural disasters, the lost of habitats greatly increase the impact of natural disasters.

At fish farms, fish are exposed to several harmful things like bacteria, viruses, and parasites. The high concentration in fish farms makes it easy for diseases to spread. These disease are passed onto other wild animals and can harm them.

Common chemicals like pesticides and drugs are used to take care of the pests and diseases that affect the fish. It is common for fish farmers to add chemicals to fish feed to help fish grow faster which passes onto to people consuming it. The water runoff of fish farms is toxic and can affect local water supply that are meant for other purposes.
